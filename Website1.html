Top 5 Saltwater Fishing Spots

Bruny Island

Known for its rugged beauty and rich waters, Bruny Island offers excellent fishing for species like flathead, bream, Australian salmon, and snapper. The sheltered bays and rocky points provide perfect spots for shore and boat fishing.

D’Entrecasteaux Channel

This calm channel between Bruny Island and mainland Tasmania is famous for its estuarine fish like flathead, bream, and giant trevally. It’s also a great spot for recreational boating and fishing from the shore.

Freycinet Peninsula / Coles Bay

This stunning area has crystal-clear waters and is a hotspot for Australian salmon, snapper, and squid. The rocky reefs and sandy beaches make it a diverse fishing environment.

Recherche Bay

Located in the southeast, Recherche Bay offers great access to kingfish, flathead, and gummy sharks. The sheltered waters and deep channels are ideal for boat fishing.

Macquarie Harbour

On Tasmania’s west coast, Macquarie Harbour is well known for its unique ecosystem and species like kingfish and salmon. It’s popular with both shore and boat anglers looking for a variety of catches.

Top 5 Freshwater Fishing Spots

Lake St Clair

Tasmania’s deepest freshwater lake, Lake St Clair is famous for its wild brown trout and rainbow trout. Surrounded by stunning wilderness, it’s perfect for anglers seeking both trophy fish and breathtaking scenery.

Great Lake

One of the largest lakes in Tasmania, Great Lake is a trout angler’s dream. It’s stocked regularly with brown and rainbow trout, and its expansive waters offer plenty of room for boat fishing.

Arthurs Lake

Known for its excellent trout fishing, Arthurs Lake is a popular spot for fly fishing and trolling. The lake is stocked with rainbow and brown trout, attracting anglers from across Australia.

Mersey River

Flowing through northern Tasmania, the Mersey River provides great freshwater fishing for brown trout and Atlantic salmon. It’s accessible for both bank fishing and small boats.

Tyenna River

Located in the Derwent Valley, the Tyenna River is famous for fly fishing, especially for wild brown trout. Its clear waters and natural setting make it a favorite among anglers seeking a peaceful fishing experience.
